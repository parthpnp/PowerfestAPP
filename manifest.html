MANIFEST

Corruption is an issue which not only affects each and every individual but also hampers the economic growth of the nation. The basic objective of the Right to Information Act has been to empower the citizens, promote transparency and accountability in the working of the government, contain corruption and make our democracy work for the people in real sense.

It is high time for the next step, the next technological move, the move which is going to change the course of Indian history. Techfest presents Manifest, which aims at presenting an ideal government website which would set a benchmark for bringing a change and making government sectors more transparent resulting in a better governance.

Vision

VISION:Â· Increase the transparency in the current system. The main motto behind this is to work against corruption bringing together technology and government to increase the transparency in the present government processes hindering activities of corruption in many cases.

Example of its usage: There is a common man, let us name him Mr. X and he has his file with an official Mr. A. Every official has a number of files with him every day.

After the change: If all the list of files with the order at every govt officer is computerized and made transparent to outer world, it becomes difficult for Mr. A to manipulate the order or any details in the application of Mr. X after taking money from him for the same. Which gives no opportunity for Mr. A to take a bribe.

Above mentioned is the small scale application of it.

STRUCTURE

Modal Header

Duration:

The competition will continue nearly for three months and registration will be open up to 15th November, 2015. Those who registered on or before 29th October were supposed to give the updates of their solution, the extent up to which they have designed the solutions and the difficulties being faced on or before 31st October. Those who missed the progress report submission are supposed send a request mail to manifest@techfest.org with the subject 'Request to accept the 1st progress report - Team id'. Those who registered after 29th October can directly go for Final Submission.
The final deadline for the competition is 15th November 2015.

Team Structure:

One can register with a maximum of four members in the team. Ideal team suggestion would be 2 people who are interested in social issue and 2 people who are interested in coding and design. But note that the above mentioned division is not mandatory and is open for the participants to decide their combination.

General Rules for Submission:

Registration Procedure:
A maximum of 4 students can register to form a team. The team must specify a team representative (TR) who will then be the point of contact for Manifest from that team.

Duties of the team:
1. Pick up a department from the 6 departments which we gave as a choice.
2. Study the present government website of that department perfectly and find where the present website needs improvement.

Round 1:
Write an abstract of where the present website can be improved and why it is necessary to incorporate the change. The abstract may contain pictures of the present situation of the website and what you expect it to be for more efficiency.
The report should include all the following details: Details of Team member - Team ID, Full Name, Name of college, City, Email id and contact number.
Department Selected:
Government Website verified (link is to be provided):
Improvements suggested: ( to be mentioned pointwise)

Those who registered on or before 29th October were supposed to give the updates of their solution, the extent up to which they have designed the solutions and the difficulties being faced on or before 31st October. Those who missed the progress report submission are supposed send a request mail to manifest@techfest.org with the subject 'Request to accept the 1st progress report - Team id'. Those who registered after 29th October can directly go for Final Submission.
The subject of the mail should be Team ID. For example: Round 1-Team ID - Municipal Services. Do not forget to mention the team ID in the subject of the mail and the report. The following details should be followed in the report:

(1) All answers should be written in one report only,
(2) The report should be submitted in the pdf format only,
(3) Font: Arial and Font size: 11.

A team should send only one report per genre. If a team sends multiple entries for one genre, the one sent earliest shall be considered. All other reports shall be discarded.
Mentors will be allotted to the teams after the verification of the 1st round report.

Round 2:
1. Talk to the officials of the concerned department they pick and do proper research on where corruption is happening and what else can be implemented to increase the transparency. ( with the help of the mentor we provide)
2. Write the entire coding program for the ideal website expected.
3. The website can be written in any language of your choice: PHP, Python, JSP, etc. You will be given your own space in the server where you need to host your website. Basically you are required to have your website running for usage. We will provide proper documentation on how to do this. The details regarding this will be informed at a later stage.

The pattern for this submission will be informed after 1st round submission The solutions will be judged by the experts in the relevant fields.
