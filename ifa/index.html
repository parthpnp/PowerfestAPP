About

In this era of connectivity and high-speed Internet where we all are stepping towards a Digital India, thousands of villages and towns are still untouched by Technology and Internet. It’s time to envision a life where everyone around the globe can access the Internet and increase the knowledge about the world we live in. Why should only 40% of us enjoy being connected with everyone we want to via all forms of media available on the Internet, while 60% do not even know how to reach out to someone for help in case of emergencies – or just for sharing some good news with someone who is not around? Of course, this makes Internet for All even more needed.

Under the guidance of Global bodies as UNICEF, UNESCO, CEE, and SAYEN, who want to make a difference, Techfest is well on the way to bridge this unfathomable gap through the ‘Internet For All’ campaign

A connectivity vehicle, equipped with Exhibitions of latest science and technology to inspire school children will travel from Mumbai to Delhi spreading the much needed Digital literacy and access to the source of infinite knowledge and information, the Internet. Our motto is to provide a sweet taste of Internet to the remote rural areas and prepare them to adopt ‘Digitalisation and Internet’ along with the rest of the nation.

    Pilot Project

    To test the impact of the ‘Internet For All’ campaign, a pilot project was launched on 14th July in collaboration with the Nehru Science Center, a subdivision of the National Council of Science Museums (NCSM), Ministry of Culture. Their Mobile Science Exhibition Bus was provided with Internet connectivity and it will cover 26 schools in different villages of Nashik district of Maharashtra over a period of 60 days.
