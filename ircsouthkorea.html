IRC South Korea

It has been among the intrinsic mottos of Techfest to provide a fair and level playing ground, that is grand enough to prompt the participants to give their best. In this venture the support of our international partners has proved crucial. Hence, Techfest comes to you at UNIST, South Korea.

International Robotics Challenge is an international Challenge comprised of designing a perfectly synchronised system of robots that perform according to the rules of the game played with an utmost precision on the international arena, IRC also displays overwhelming ecstasy of proving ones supremacy over the competitor. Expertising manual handling skills as well as performing specific tasks autonomously.

Top two teams from each country will qualify and would be invited to India to compete in International Robotics Challenge Grand Finale which would be held on 26th - 28th December, 2015 at Techfest, IIT Bombay, India.

Any International team who failed to participate in any of the IRC zonals can participate in International Robotics Challenge through IRC International Open which will be held at Techfest 2015, India.

  • TASK


    1.There will be two bots, a manual bot and an autonomous bot. They need to coordinate with each other to complete the task.
    2.The autonomous bot has to solve the grid by following the white lines while avoiding the nodes and has to climb over the Inclines creating its path and.
    3.The manual bot has to walk over the bridge of sticks, transfer the block to the deposit zone, help the autonomous bot reach its final position and put the flag in the wet sand.
    4.A maximum of 6 minutes will be allotted to each participating team.

  • GAMEPLAY


    1.The manual bot starts from the manual start zone and the autonomous bot starts from the autonomous start zone.
    2.The manual bot first goes along the pit containing wooden planks. If the bot fails to cross this, it will be allowed to skip the pit but with a penalty of 25 points. After crossing the pit, the manual bot would find the block 1 that it has to transfer to deposit zone 1. It is allowed to enter the autonomous zone only for depositing the block 1 in the deposit zone 1. However it is not allowed to go through the incline and stay in the autonomous zone after depositing the block 1.
    3.Meanwhile, the autonomous bot must start from the autonomous start zone. It has to solve the grid by following the white lines, avoiding the nodes.
    4.Autonomous bot has to find the block 1 in the autonomous zone, carry it over its head and then reach to deposit zone 2 in the incline. (Note that the autonomous bot cannot cross the deposit zone 1 unless the manual bot drops the block 1 in it).
    5.Once the manual bot deposits the block 1 in the deposit zone 1, the autonomous bot can now cross it and reach the top of the incline carting the block 2. The Autonomous bot has to deposit the block 2 in deposit zone 2.
    6.The autonomous bot, after crossing the incline, has to get down from the incline to the grid.
    7.Meanwhile the manual bot has to walk beside the incline and reach to the other side of the incline.
    8.The autonomous bot has to solve the grid and reach the common point. It is supposed stop and manual bot is supposed to push the autonomous bot to the next grid position avoiding the nodes. The Manual bot should not go beyond the the permitted zone.
    9.The manual bot is supposed to collect the flag from the manual zone, go through the propeller and finally put the flag in the wet sand (figure 4) to finish the gameplay.
    10.Meanwhile the autonomous bot is supposed reach the ending point.
