CYCLOTHON

Cycling symbolises a stand for fitness and environment consciousness, and the campus of IIT Bombay with its lush green environment is an appropriate destination for the event. We expect the citizens of Mumbai to embrace the event and come out in large numbers to participate.

Event participation is open to all cycling enthusiasts regardless of their age.

This is your opportunity to ride your cycle inside IIT Bombay campus along its lakes and amidst its greenery on a 13 Km long route. Please keep checking the website for updates.

ABOUT

Many of us who enjoy cycling in early morning know how amazing it feels, the cool morning breeze filling our warm chest, the sun peeking over the horizon, the fresh smell of morning air and the empty wide streets made just for us. Cycling is the best way to achieve physical fitness and good health. It also saves a lot of fossil fuels and helps in reducing global warming. Early morning cycling burns 300% more fat than exercising in any other time of the day. But sadly most of the youth of our nation have not even tried this wonderful experience even once.

Techfest, IIT Bombay brings forth a full-fledged campaign ReCycle to promote health and fitness among the youth. ReCycle consistis of three events a college campaign, an online campaign and the final cyclothon which looks forward to bring a better health and fitness to the youth of India.

COLLEGE CAMPAIGN

Techfest, IIT Bombay reached out to over 20 colleges in Mumbai to conduct the college campaign. These college campaigns focused on involving the participants and spectators in sharing knowledge, ideas, views and finding better solutions on Global Warming through various activities. These activities included 3 events, each college was awarded given green points depending on the number of participants and winners. These events were Spinning Wheel Game, Quote Tree and Plant a Tree.

Spinning Wheel Game was further subdivided into four tasks. Participants had to perform the task depending on the position of the pointer where it stopped on the spinning wheel. These tasks were making of a bag out of a rag t-shirt, slogan on ‘Go Green’, brainstorming on improving the existing means of controlling global warming and identifying which type of waste goes in which coloured dustbin.

Quote Tree consisted of spectators and participants writing quotes about global warming on the leaves of a tree on the flex.

Plant a Tree involved planting a sapling by the Dean of the college and students together symbolising the first step in stopping Global Warming.
